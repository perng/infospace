## The exponential series
*The Evening Lecture Hall · Center Board - the Exponential*

```latex
e^{x} \;=\; \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n}}{n!} \;=\; 1 + x + \frac{x^{2}}{2!} + \frac{x^{3}}{3!} + \cdots
```

*Spoken:* e to the x equals the sum from n equals zero to infinity of x to the n over n factorial; that is, one plus x plus x squared over two factorial plus x cubed over three factorial, and so on.

**Speaker notes:** The exponential as a power series: every term, every x. Let the chalk write itself out; the factorials are doing the convergence work.

## Sine and cosine, the same way
*The Evening Lecture Hall · Right Board - Sine and Cosine*

```latex
\begin{aligned} \cos x &= 1 - \frac{x^{2}}{2!} + \frac{x^{4}}{4!} - \cdots \\[4pt] \sin x &= x - \frac{x^{3}}{3!} + \frac{x^{5}}{5!} - \cdots \end{aligned}
```

*Spoken:* cosine x equals one minus x squared over two factorial plus x to the fourth over four factorial, minus and so on; sine x equals x minus x cubed over three factorial plus x to the fifth over five factorial, minus and so on.

**Speaker notes:** Same machine, two more functions. Point at the alternating signs and the split between even and odd powers - that split is the whole proof.

## Substitute ix
*The Evening Lecture Hall · West Wall - the Threshold*

### Through the Board

Substitute ix into the exponential series and the terms split: the even powers are cosine, the odd powers are i times sine.

The board is only a surface. Step through it, into the plane where these series live.

**Speaker notes:** Do the substitution out loud: i squared is minus one, so even powers alternate sign (cosine) and odd powers keep an i (sine). Then walk to the center board and step through.

## Euler's formula
*The Math Void · Glass - the Substitution*

```latex
e^{ix} \;=\; \cos x \,+\, i\,\sin x
```

*Spoken:* e to the i x equals cosine x plus i times sine x.

**Speaker notes:** We are inside the plane now. The substitution's result stands in glass: e to the i x is a point with coordinates cosine x and sine x.

## The unit circle
*The Math Void · The Living Unit Circle*

### The Unit Circle

Every value of e to the i theta lies at distance one from zero. The exponential does not explode along this axis - it orbits.

**Speaker notes:** Watch the point orbit: modulus one, forever. Imaginary exponents rotate instead of growing - that is the geometric heart of it.

## The identity
*The Math Void · Glass - the Identity*

```latex
e^{i\pi} + 1 \;=\; 0
```

*Spoken:* e to the i pi, plus one, equals zero.

**Speaker notes:** Set x to pi: half a turn. Land on minus one, add one, get zero. Let the equation breathe - this is the destination promised at the first board.

## Back through the board
*The Math Void · The Return Gate*

### Back Through the Board

Half a turn around the circle is pi. Land at minus one, add one, and arrive at zero.

Carry the line home: five constants, one circle, no coincidence.
